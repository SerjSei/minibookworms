Leo's Birthday

Click next to start the story.

Leo the lion was very excited when he woke up. Today was the day: it was his 7th birthday. As Leo got up and looked out the window, his smile suddenly dropped. It was raining in the Mighty Jungle. Oh no, Leo thought. We were supposed to go celebrate my birthday on the beach. How are we going to do that now?

Page 1

After eating breakfast, it was time for Leo’s friends to arrive, and soon someone knocked on the door. In stepped Polly the parrot and Sally the snake. Polly was wearing a yellow rain jacket and boots, and Sally had a red umbrella.

Page 2

“Oh, I know what we should do. Let’s bake a cake!” Sally said.
“We could have a baking competition,” Polly suggested. “That way everyone can make something they like.”
“That’s an amazing idea,” Leo agreed.

Page 3

The friends hurried into the kitchen, ready to turn Leo’s birthday into a rainy-day bake-off. Pots and pans clanged, flour dusted the air like soft snow, and colorful bowls of sugar, eggs, and sprinkles were set out on the counters.

Page 5

“Alright, what’s everyone going to make?” Leo asked, tying a big apron around himself. It was so big it almost touched his toes!
“I’m making chocolate banana cupcakes!” Polly chirped. “They’re delicious and...parrot-approved!” She gave a little twirl, flapping her bright wings.

Page 6

Sally slid over to her bowl, grinning. “I’m making slithery cinnamon rolls! They’ll be gooey and warm—perfect for a rainy day.”
Leo scratched his mane, thinking hard. “I’ll make a big, tall rainbow cake! With seven layers, one for each year!” he said with a grin.

Page 7

Soon, the kitchen was filled with the smell of melted chocolate, cinnamon, and the soft hum of the oven. Polly squawked as she added extra chocolate chips to her cupcakes. Sally twirled in excitement as she rolled up her cinnamon dough, dusting each layer with sugar and spice.

Page 8

Leo carefully layered his rainbow cake, stacking colors one by one until it reached as tall as his nose. He licked a bit of frosting from his paw and smiled. “This is going to be the best cake ever!”
Just then, a tiny knock sounded at the door. “Who could that be?” Leo wondered.

Page 9

Leo opened the door to find Ollie the owl standing there with a little box tied up in a golden ribbon. Ollie blinked his big, wise eyes and said, “I heard it’s someone’s birthday! I brought a special treat—homemade honey muffins!”
Leo’s face lit up. “Oh, thank you, Ollie! Come in and join the bake-off!”

Page 11

As Ollie settled in with his muffin batter, they all laughed, chatted, and shared their baking secrets. Ollie even taught them how to fold batter gently so it stayed fluffy. The kitchen was warm and cozy, filled with the soft patter of rain outside and the happy sounds of mixing, stirring, and laughter.

Page 12

Finally, after a lot of waiting, their treats were ready. They set up a cozy corner with a big picnic blanket, decorated with all the yummy goodies they had baked.
Leo’s eyes sparkled as he looked around. “Even though we couldn’t go to the beach, this is the best birthday I could ask for,” he said. His friends cheered, and Polly put a little paper crown on his head.

Page 13

“Make a wish, Leo!” Sally said, handing him a cupcake with a tiny, glowing candle.
Leo closed his eyes, took a deep breath, and thought of his wish. Then he blew out the candle, feeling warm and happy. When he opened his eyes, he was met with a huge group hug from his friends.

Page 14

The rain continued to fall outside, but inside, the Mighty Jungle felt warm, bright, and filled with laughter. And Leo knew, as he munched on his rainbow cake, that sometimes the best celebrations are the ones where friends make the day magical, no matter the weather. 

Page 15

And from that day on, Leo knew that rain or shine, the best birthdays were the ones spent with friends who knew just how to make any day special.
